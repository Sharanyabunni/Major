About Our Research

Our Mission

Advancing cardiovascular diagnostics through innovative machine learning approaches

Pioneering Cardiovascular Diagnostics

Our research team is dedicated to developing innovative, non-invasive methods for early detection and risk assessment of coronary artery disease (CAD) and acute coronary syndrome (ACS).

Through the application of ensemble machine learning techniques to focused carotid ultrasound imaging, we aim to provide healthcare professionals with powerful tools to improve patient outcomes and reduce the burden of cardiovascular disease.

  • Developing non-invasive diagnostic tools for early CAD detection
  • Applying advanced machine learning to medical imaging analysis
  • Validating our approach through rigorous clinical studies
  • Collaborating with leading medical institutions worldwide

Research Timeline

Key milestones in our journey to develop advanced CAD prediction tools

January 2024

Initial Research

Began investigating the relationship between carotid ultrasound features and coronary artery disease, establishing the foundational hypothesis for our work.

March 2024

Algorithm Development

Developed initial machine learning algorithms for feature extraction and classification of carotid ultrasound images, with promising preliminary results.

June 2024

Ensemble Model Creation

Designed and implemented ensemble machine learning approach, combining Random Forest, KNN, XGBoost, and Naive Bayes algorithms to achieve higher accuracy and robustness.

September 2024

Feature Engineering

Refined our feature extraction techniques to include color histograms, Hu moments, Haralick texture features, and color texture analysis for comprehensive image representation.

December 2024

System Refinement

Refined the prediction system based on validation results, achieving 95% overall accuracy with balanced precision and recall metrics.

February 2025

Platform Development

Developed the CardioPredict platform to make our technology accessible to healthcare providers worldwide, with a focus on usability and integration.

April 2025

Expanded Applications

Currently exploring expanded applications of our technology, including prediction of other cardiovascular conditions and integration with additional imaging modalities.
